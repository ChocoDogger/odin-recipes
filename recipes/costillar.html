Costillar Argentino Recipe

Description

Costillar Argentino, or Argentine beef ribs, is a traditional dish prepared during an asado, the Argentine barbecue. The ribs are seasoned with a coarse mix of salt, pepper, sweet chili, and oregano, then grilled slowly over a wood fire, allowing the flavors to develop and the meat to become tender and juicy.

Ingredients

  • 1 rack of beef ribs (Costillar)
  • Coarse salt
  • Ground black pepper
  • Sweet chili powder
  • Dried oregano
  • Optional: garlic powder, parsley powder, or curry powder

Steps

  1. Prepare the seasoning mix by combining coarse salt, ground black pepper, sweet chili powder, and dried oregano. Optionally, add garlic powder, parsley powder, or curry powder to taste.

  2. Generously season both sides of the beef ribs with the spice mix, rubbing it into the meat to ensure even coverage.

  3. Preheat your grill, preferably using wood or charcoal, to a medium-low heat. The ideal temperature is around 250°F (120°C).

  4. Place the seasoned ribs on the grill, bone side down. Cook slowly for several hours, maintaining a consistent temperature, until the meat is tender and has developed a rich, smoky flavor.

  5. Once cooked to your desired doneness, remove the ribs from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.
